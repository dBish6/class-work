Taking Care of Betta Fishes

Food/Diet

Determining your Betta fish's diet can be difficult. There are many brands and varieties of Betta fish food available, but of course some of them are low-quality and not best suitable for your fish. Some live foods is some of the healthiest way to feed you're Betta fish. The most popular live foods include brine shrimp, mosquito larvae, and bloodworms. Although, watching your Betta fish eat something live can be exicting, this can put you at risk of bringing parasites into your tank; just try not to feed him anything you find outside.

If your Betta fish doesn't like live food or you just like other options you can go with Betta fish flakes or pellets. Important to note, that your betta fish are carnivores that need a very high protein diet. Fish flakes and pellets is the most common way to feed your fish, so when buying make sure it is specifically meant for betta fish, has high protein and other nutrients that helps the fish thrive. Also, feeding your fish the same thing for forever can sertily keep them alive, but just like us they want a wide range of food to be healthy.

Frozen food is also a good option, just like live food, but the foods i mentioned frozen, this example is more safe then live food. So, I would recommend experimenting with different food types for best results.

Water Quality

Betta fish in their history could tolerate harsh water conditions, but keeping your fish healthy by not catching an illness, you should think otherwise. Betta's prefer calm water and because they are a tropical fish, they need the water temperature to be warm. If you're room temperature isn't in the tropics, a tank heater is a great alternative. The tank's water which is best for their health is between 76° and 85°F. Be advised the size of the heater according to how big your tank is; a mini heater for small containers and so on. Also, clean your tanks!
