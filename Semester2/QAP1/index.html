Betta "Siamese fighting Fishes"

By: David Bishop
January 29, 2022

About Betta Fishes

The Siamese fighting fish or most notably known as the Betta fish is a beautiful fish. Although, these fish are cruel in nature, they can make a lovely friend as a pet. The name Siamese fighting fish is the creature's scientific name, this name was givin' because Betta fish fight other Betta fish. However, only male Betta fish fight each other, they do this to establish a territory because they want everything to themselves, food, shelter, whatever they want. So, recommending a cute roomate for him will be a difficult task, yet if they have proper health and plenty of room to move around, this is possible. On the other hand, where the name Betta came from was from an ancient warrior tribe in Thailand. In fact, they thought of these fish as "warrior fish", thus spawning a so called sport where the fishes fight. Rather then betting on who is the last fish standing, spectators would actually bet on what fish was more brave. What a history for a cute little tropical fish, huh?

The Betta was first discovered in Southeast Asia and the fish begin it's popularity in the mid-1800s. The harsh conditions of Thailand, the fish didn't really find a suitable home; making their homes in ditches, flood plains, etc. Constant flooding from storms and other natural occurrences, made the fishes move from place to place more often. The Betta's adapted and became more accustomed to these conditions, thus becoming a labyrith fish. What is a Labrith fish? These fishes have the ability to breathe with their gills, just like mostly every fish, but they possess a unique abilty to breathe oxygen. Following this, Betta fish can inhale the air, but they can only do this for short periods of time, this helped them tremendously thoughout their existence. Though, these "beutiful warriors" (was another name given to these fish in the 1800s) could tolerate these conditions, they still like clean water quality. With appropriate water changes and proper water temperature beacuese these fish are also tropical fish (more information on how to take care of Betta fishes via the side panel, "Taking Care of Betta Fishes").

These pets are fairly common, one of the most popular pet fish today, but some are quite more rare then others. With around 73 known recognized species as of today, each fish is unique by their species type with a wide range of colors and fin types (the most notable types can be accessed via the side panel, "Types of Betta Fishes", for more information).

Facts:

  • Most Betta fish live for 2 - 3 years.
  • During times of betta fish breeding, male Betta's build a bubble nest at the surface to attract a female. That's if you ever wondered why they make bubbles at the top of your tank.
  • The correct way to pronounce Betta is not like what mostly everyone says it is, "bay-tuh". It is actually pronouced "bet-tah" originating from the ancient warrior tribe talked about.
